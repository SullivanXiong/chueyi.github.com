First Inspiration

“Who is Playing the Guqin” is one of the greatest interactive media sites I’ve ever come across and will be using for my final project inspiration. The website has a unique painterly style that evokes traditional Chinese aesthetics. One key aspect about this site is that there are hardly any geometric shapes which makes it identifiable. It takes you through a mountainous journey with a character that elegantly jumps from mountaintop to mountaintop and even has a river section which changes the perspective of the interaction.

I cannot read Chinese but the icons and hand gestures that are present on the screen makes it user friendly to navigate, but I cannot truly understand the purpose of the website. This made me realize the importance of including symbols and icons here and there to guide users. The website needs a realignment of some of the buttons that prompts the interactive events to start up because it took a while for me to notice them. The monochromatism of the website also didn’t make it easy to point out the buttons.
